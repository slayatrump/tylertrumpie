Tyler Trumpie

Who I am

    I am Tyler Trumpie. I am currently enrolled in a 3 year bachelor degree in computer science with a specialty in game design and simulation at Davenport University.
    I have always been fascinated by technology, but didn't pursue the field until my first programming class in my sophomore year of high school, Java Programming I.
    Ever since that Java class, I have been interested in the ins and outs of coding. I decided to go for game design because games give people not only an escape from real life, but for some a job as a streamer, a passion for a career, or just another way to be entertained. Video games have been constantly evolving, and I have been along for the ride, hoping to take the wheel and drive home a new game that will leave an impression on anyone who plays it. I don't study game design because I think it is a good career, I study game design because it is a great career for me. I am following my dream, and I am excited to see where it takes me.
    If you would like to contact me, email me at tylertrumpie@gmail.com.

Personality

  I am a guy that lightens the mood, be it through funny comments or making a fool of myself. I love to make others know that they are loved and known by at least someone. I am a people pleaser; I find joy in making others happy. That plays a huge role in my work ethic. I want to make something that will bring joy into whoever sees it.
  My creativity works off of a foundation. Whenever an idea is presented to me, I can bring up the mechanics, characters, levels, and skill needed for the game. I love to theorycraft, be it a new game, balancing a mechanic, or adding something new to an existing game.

Interests

   I love programming and logic, which made me choose my career path in computer science.
    I love video games, not just as a hobby, but also as a way to spark my brain with creative ideas. For instance, I find myself taking a look at existing mechanics in a game and dreaming up ways in which that mechanic could be more interesting or integral to a specific playstyle.
    Mechanics creation and testing is another interest I discovered in high school. The rewarding experience after successfully implementing a mechanic I thought was impossible or very difficult is something that pushes me along this career path.
